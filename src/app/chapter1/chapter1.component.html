Cryptography Defined

"Cryptography is the science of secret writing"

Cryptography is concerned with designing algorithms to provide confidentiality, integrity, and authentication. Well-designed cryptographic protocols protect against eavesdropping, tampering, and impersonation.

Keyed vs. Keyless Schemes

Keyless Cryptosystem

Performs encryption/decryption without a secret key.

Symmetric (Secret-Key)

Same key for encryption and decryption (fast, but key distribution is challenging).

Asymmetric (Public-Key)

Separate public/private keys (computationally intensive, ideal for digital signatures).

Hash Functions

Map arbitrary data to fixed-length digests, ensuring integrity and enabling authentication.

Block & Stream Ciphers

Block Ciphers

Encrypt fixed-size blocks (e.g., 64 bits) as a permutation under a secret key.

Pros: Diffusion across block, tamper detection if block length changes.
Cons: Must buffer entire block, error propagation within block.

Stream Ciphers

Encrypt data one unit at a time using a keystream.

Pros: Very fast, low error propagation.
Cons: Lower diffusion, vulnerable to insertion and selective modification.

Breaking Ciphers

Cryptanalysis is the art of recovering plaintext or keys. Theoretical or practical feasibility depends on algorithm strength and information available to the attacker.

"A cryptosystem should be secure even if everything about the system, except the key, is public knowledge."
